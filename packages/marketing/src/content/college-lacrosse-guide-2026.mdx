The 2026 NCAA lacrosse season is here. Whether you're a die-hard or tuning in for the first time, this guide covers everything you need to follow the action this spring.

## How to Watch

### Television & Streaming

- **ESPN / ESPN2** — Marquee matchups throughout the season, plus full NCAA Tournament coverage
- **ESPN+** — The hub for college lacrosse. Most regular season games live here
- **ESPNU** — Select midweek and weekend games
- **Big Ten Network** — Conference games for Maryland, Ohio State, Rutgers, Penn State, Johns Hopkins, Michigan
- **ACC Network** — Duke, Virginia, Syracuse, Notre Dame conference games

### Free & Alternative Streams

- **FloSports** — Patriot League, select smaller conference matchups
- **ESPN+** is effectively required if you want to follow the season seriously

## Men's Division I Preview

### Teams to Watch

**Virginia** — The Cavaliers remain the standard. Deep roster, elite coaching under Lars Tiffany, and a program that reloads rather than rebuilds. Perennial title contender.

**Duke** — Always in the conversation. Duke's combination of recruiting power and offensive firepower makes them dangerous in any bracket.

**Maryland** — The Terps bring Big Ten physicality and are perpetually in the title hunt. John Tillman's program hasn't missed the tournament in over a decade.

**Syracuse** — The Orange have been knocking on the door. A loaded attack and the weight of a legendary program behind them.

**Notre Dame** — Kevin Corrigan's squad is always well-coached and capable of a deep run. Don't sleep on the Irish.

**Georgetown** — Consistently competitive and always a tough out in the Big East.

**Penn State** — Rising program. Jeff Tambroni has built something real in Happy Valley.

### Conference Breakdown

**Big Ten** — The deepest conference in the sport. Maryland, Johns Hopkins, Ohio State, Rutgers, Penn State, and Michigan all compete at a high level. Every game is a battle.

**ACC** — Star power and tradition. Virginia, Duke, Syracuse, and Notre Dame make this conference appointment viewing.

**Big East** — Georgetown leads, but the conference is competitive top to bottom. Denver and Villanova are factors.

**Ivy League** — Don't underestimate the Ivies. Yale, Cornell, and Princeton regularly produce tournament teams with some of the smartest lacrosse you'll see.

**Patriot League** — Loyola Maryland and Army are the perennial powers, but the league sends legitimate contenders to the NCAA Tournament.

## Women's Division I Preview

### Teams to Watch

**Boston College** — Acme Bowen has built a juggernaut. The Eagles have been dominant and are the team to beat again.

**Northwestern** — Kelly Amonte Hiller's program is always elite. The Wildcats reload every year with top-tier talent.

**North Carolina** — The Tar Heels are a force. Jenny Levy's squad combines speed, skill, and competitive fire.

**Syracuse** — Kayla Treanor is building something special. The Orange are ascending fast.

**Maryland** — The Terps are always in the mix. Too much talent and tradition to count out.

### Conference Landscape

The ACC and Big Ten dominate women's lacrosse much like the men's side. The draw rule changes and shot clock have made the women's game faster and more exciting than ever.

## What Makes College Lacrosse Special

If you're coming from the [[PLL]] or [[NLL]], college lacrosse hits different:

- **Atmosphere** — Nothing beats a packed college stadium on a spring afternoon. Homecomings, rivalries, student sections
- **Parity** — Upsets happen constantly. Any team can beat any team on a given day
- **Emotion** — Seniors playing their last game. Underdogs making a run. The stakes feel real because they are
- **Style diversity** — Every program plays differently. Virginia's methodical possession game vs Syracuse's up-tempo chaos vs a Patriot League team grinding it out
- **The Tournament** — Single elimination. One and done. The NCAA Tournament is the best event in lacrosse, full stop
