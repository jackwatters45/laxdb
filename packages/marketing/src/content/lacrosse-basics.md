**Basics** is the entry point for players who are learning the sport for the first time.

This section should answer the questions a new player is likely to have before their first practice, first game, or first season.

## Audience

Brand new players who may not know the rules, equipment, positions, terminology, or what to expect at practice.

## Start here

### [[What Is Lacrosse?]]

Objective, teams, scoring, flow of play, and the difference between boys'/men's, girls'/women's, box, and field lacrosse.

### [[Lacrosse Equipment]]

What a new player needs, what each item does, and what may vary by version of the sport.

### [[Stick Basics]]

Parts of the stick, how to hold it, pocket basics, strong hand/off hand, and why stick protection matters.

### [[The Lacrosse Field]]

Goals, crease, restraining lines, midfield line, sidelines, end lines, substitution area, and faceoff area.

### [[Lacrosse Positions]]

Attack, midfield, defense, goalie, faceoff specialist, LSM, and short-stick defensive midfielder.

### [[Basic Lacrosse Rules]]

Possession, boundaries, checking, fouls, offsides, crease rules, restarts, and penalties at a high level.

### [[Core Lacrosse Skills]]

Cradling, catching, throwing, ground balls, shooting, dodging, defending, and communication.

### [[First Practice Guide]]

What to bring, what to expect, beginner etiquette, and a simple before-practice checklist.

### [[Beginner Lacrosse Glossary]]

Plain-English definitions for common terms across the beginner section.

## Content goals

- Use plain language.
- Avoid assuming prior knowledge.
- Define terms the first time they appear.
- Prioritize what a new player needs to know now over exhaustive detail.
- Link outward to deeper wiki pages once those exist.

## Suggested content convention

The current marketing wiki routes use flat content slugs under `packages/marketing/src/content`, so this section should use flat files like `lacrosse-basics.md` and `stick-basics.md` instead of nested files. If we later add nested wiki routing, these can move into a `basics/` folder.

- Should box basics live here or become a sibling section?
